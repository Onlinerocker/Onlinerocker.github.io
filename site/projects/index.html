Raymarched Cel-Shaded Primitives (GLSL)


These primitives are rendered in real-time entirely within a GLSL pixel shader. This is a achieved through an algorithm known as Raymarching. Put simply, rays are marched in the direction of each pixel from the camera's eye until they intersect with an object. Upon intersection, color is calculated using a lighting equation. In this case, colors are restricted to discrete color "bands". This achieves the "cel-shaded" lighting aesthetic. Intersection is determined by signed-distance functions (often abbreviated SDF).
